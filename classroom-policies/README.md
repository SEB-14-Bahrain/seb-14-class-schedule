On the final day of project week, everyone will present their project to the class.

## Course Policies

#### Homework 
* Each day you will be given a homework assignment that is due the next morning at 9 AM. 
* You must complete 80% of these homeworks to graduate the class.
* They are graded on a 👍 or 👎 basis -- either they are complete or not. Whether they are complete or not depends on the effort you put in. They don't have to be 100% correct, but you do need to show that you put time into getting the correct answer.

#### Plagiarism and Effort

We have a plagiarism policy in place to create a healthy classroom culture where students prioritize their learning above all other goals for the duration of the cohort.  We want this class to be a space where it is **safe to fail**. Making mistakes is part of becoming a developer. If someone else does the work for you, including an AI tool, you lose the chance to build the skills you came here to learn.

**Plagiarism means submitting work that is not truly your own.**

This includes:

* Asking another person to complete your assignment for you
* Copying another student’s code and submitting it as your own
* Copying code from the internet without understanding it
* Using AI to generate a full solution and submitting it as your own work
* Submitting code that you cannot explain
* Hiding the fact that you used outside help

You must understand and be able to explain your submissions.

Plagiarized submissions will not be graded and may lead to further action, including removal from the program.

**Responsible AI Use**

AI tools are common in modern software development. In this course, you may use AI as a **learning assistant**, but not as a replacement for your own work.

A simple rule:

> AI may help you think, but your fingers must still write the code.

## How to Succeed

- Be present and pay attention
- Ask questions.  Those two things are like 90% of your success right there.
- Trust the the process.   It may feel as though you're not "learning" enough during the lectures.  This is not where you should expect to grasp the concepts in this program, in fact the lectures will make up a relatively small part of your learning.  Taking time to practice with labs will reinforce what you heard in the lectures, but project weeks are where the deep learning really happen.
- Be prepared to work very hard.  The instructors are your biggest cheerleaders and will provide support but we cannot do the work for you.
- You'll be learning, growing, failing, and succeeding **together** for the next 12 weeks.  Teamwork will be paramount to your success as a student and beyond. Programming is a teamwork-driven profession. You will learn more from each other than from the instructors!
- You will be uncomfortable. Regularly.
  * Don't be too hard on yourself.
  * You will probably feel somewhat lost on this week's topic, feel more comfortable with last week lessons, and notice that a few weeks ago looks easy.
  * The pace of this course is designed to push you past your limits - **this course is like drinking water from a firehose**.  There is more material than you can actually absorb in one go.  That is why there is repetition - we hear something in a lecture.  Then we engage with the same content, but this time we're practicing it in a lab.  Then put in another rep when we make our projects.  By that time the material is sinking in and you will finally begin to feel comfortable with the material.
  * The brain is like a muscle; sometimes it gets sore.
  * Struggling with the material doesn't mean you aren't learning; on the contrary, it reflects meaningful engagement with the material.
- Asking for help is what students find hardest. It is also what ultimately determines whether or not students will be successful in this class.  Without fail, the students who do not ask for help do the poorest in the program.  Don't forget that your fellow cohort members are a great resource when you need support and you too should lend a hand when you can.  Share what you know and ask what you don't!

### The Emotional Cycle of Change

![Emotional Cycle of Change](https://s-media-cache-ak0.pinimg.com/736x/3c/f4/fa/3cf4faeb2078f4f4fbb9265444bde2e0.jpg)

**Important Video:** [The power of belief: Mindset & Success](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pN34FNbOKXc)

> How do you think GROWTH MINDSET could come into play for a bootcamp student? 

This may be the most mentally challenging thing you've ever done - the first time you've been bad at something in a long time. You will fail and struggle at times, but that's important. We don't succeed despite challenges - we succeed because of challenges.

* Unfortunately, this can hurt. Just like exercise can make you sore and tired, so should learning. Try to enjoy the burn knowing it is making you better.
* We anticipate that you will run into hardships. When you do, that's when it is time to really challenge yourself and buckle down. Don't quit! Embrace the fact that the challenge will make you a better coder. Endure the struggle to grow and develop your mind.

Programming is a mindset. It's about confidence and being open to failure. It's about knowing when to climb from the bottom of the cycle and being able to say, "Eh, I'm going to try this thing, even though I don't really understand it. AND I'm not going to get attached to it in case it doesn't work and I have to erase all my code and try something completely different."

The growth mindset here is understanding that even when something doesn't work, it will still make you a better coder! 

Throughout the course many students say something along the lines of "I don't know how to do that." Many of you certainly will. If you do, however, just add "...yet" to the end of that sentence.

#### Embrace failure.

* Here we learn by doing, and things don't always (and usually won't) go right the first time.
* This is normal. Expect it.
* Fail often and learn from it.
  
#### There's no winning or losing.

* This is a non-competitive environment.
* You get out what you put in.
* Everyone enters and leaves at different levels. Try to compare you with you only.
* Use your classmates' work as inspiration to improve your own!

**With that in mind, the final and most important policy is to treat everyone with dignity and respect!**
